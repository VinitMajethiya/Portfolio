# CONTENT.md — Portfolio Copy & Content Guide

> This document defines every word, section, and story beat on the portfolio. Fill in the `[PLACEHOLDERS]` with your real details before building. The tone is already defined — you just need to supply the facts.

---

## Tone of Voice

### The Voice Is
- First person, always
- Conversational but never casual-sloppy
- Honest — no corporate buzzwords
- Specific — "I built X because Y broke" not "I'm passionate about innovation"
- Slightly cinematic — we're telling a story, not listing achievements

### The Voice Is NOT
- "Highly motivated self-starter"
- "Passionate about technology"
- Third person ("John is a developer who...")
- Overly modest or overly boastful
- Jargon-heavy

### Test Every Line
Ask: *Would a real human being actually say this out loud?* If no — rewrite.

## Section 01 — Hero

### Purpose
First impression. Must stop the scroll. Must not say "Hi, I'm [Name]."

### Opening Line Options (pick one or write your own in this spirit)

**Option A — Identity-forward:**
> "I build things that matter — with code, with teams, and occasionally with junk lying around the house."

**Option B — Contrast-driven:**
> "By day, I train models and debug APIs. By night, I'm three chapters deep in a thriller I've definitely read before."

**Option C — Direct & bold:**
> "Most portfolios tell you what I can do. This one will tell you who I am."

**→ Recommended:** Option A, but make it yours. Add your actual name underneath in small `--font-mono` text.

### Hero Sub-line
One line. 10–15 words. What you do + what you're looking for.
```
[YOUR NAME] · Final Year Student · Seeking Internships in Data/AI & Web Dev
```

### Hero CTA
Two buttons:
- Primary: `See My Work` → scrolls to Projects
- Ghost: `Get In Touch` → scrolls to Contact

## Section 02 — About

### Purpose
Not a bio. A story. The kind of thing you'd say if someone asked "so tell me about yourself" and you actually answered honestly.

### Structure

**Opening (1–2 sentences — the hook):**
Write something unexpected. Not where you go to school. Something true and specific.

*Example template (replace with your reality):*
> "I became interested in machine learning the same way I got into guitar — I heard something that didn't make sense to me, and I couldn't leave it alone until it did."

**Middle (2–3 sentences — the journey):**
How did you get here? What problem or curiosity drove you toward tech? Connect to something human — a moment, a frustration, a project that surprised you.

```
[FILL IN: Your actual story. What made you choose this field?
What's a moment that clicked for you? Be specific — not vague.]
```

**The leader part (1–2 sentences):**
> "I've always been the person who gets pulled into running things — not because I planned for it, but because I care too much to let things fall apart."

Connect this to your proudest leadership moment:
```
[FILL IN: Brief mention of the team/event you led. What was it? 
What did pulling it off feel like?]
```

**Closing (1 sentence — the forward look):**
> "Right now, I'm looking for a place where I can do work that actually changes something — and learn fast doing it."

## Section 04 — Projects

### Purpose
Show what you've built, why it mattered, and what you learned. 3 projects minimum, 5 maximum.

### Per-Project Template
Fill this in for each project:

```
PROJECT TITLE: [Name]
TYPE TAG: [Data Project / Web App / ML Model / Tool / etc.]

HEADLINE (1 line, punchy):
[Not "An e-commerce website." Say: "A recommendation engine that learned what my hostel friends actually watch."]

THE PROBLEM (1–2 sentences):
[What broke, frustrated, or didn't exist that made you build this?]

WHAT I BUILT (2–3 sentences):
[What does it actually do? Be specific. Avoid vague words like "platform" or "solution."]

WHAT I LEARNED (1–2 sentences):
[The most honest thing. What broke? What surprised you? What would you do differently?]

BUILT WITH:
[Stack tags: Python · Scikit-learn · Flask · React, etc.]

LINKS:
- GitHub: [link or "private repo"]
- Live: [link or "coming soon" or "local only"]
```

### Project Ordering
Lead with the project you're most proud of — not the most technically impressive. The one that has the best story.

## Section 06 — Leadership Moment

### Purpose
One spotlight story. Not a list of positions held. A single moment that shows what kind of person you are under pressure.

### Format: The Story

**Setup (1–2 sentences):**
```
[FILL IN: What was the event/team? What was the context?
How did you end up leading it?]
```

**The challenge (1–2 sentences):**
```
[FILL IN: What almost went wrong? What was the hard part?
This is the part that makes it real — don't skip it.]
```

**The outcome (1–2 sentences):**
```
[FILL IN: How did it go? What did it feel like when it worked?
One specific detail that makes it real.]
```

**The reflection (1 sentence):**
> "That taught me that the job of a leader is mostly to be the person who doesn't panic."

*(Or write your own — something you genuinely took away from it.)*

## Section 07 — Contact

### Purpose
Make it easy and human. Not a cold form.

### Opening Line
Something warm, not mechanical.

*Example:*
> "If you're building something interesting and need someone who'll care about it as much as you do — let's talk."

Or more direct:
> "I'm actively looking for internships starting [MONTH, YEAR]. My inbox is open."

### Contact Options
```
Email:    [your@email.com]
LinkedIn: [linkedin.com/in/yourhandle]
GitHub:   [github.com/yourhandle]
```

**Optional:** Add a one-line note about response time.
> "I check email every day and respond within 24 hours."

### What NOT to Include in Contact
- A contact form with 6 fields
- "Let's synergize"
- "I look forward to hearing from you"
